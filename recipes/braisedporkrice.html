Braised Pork Rice

Description

Taiwan's braised pork rice, known locally as Luroufan (滷肉飯), is a beloved comfort food that epitomizes the essence of Taiwanese cuisine. This savory dish features finely chopped pork belly, slow-cooked in a rich and aromatic sauce made from soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, and a medley of spices including star anise and cinnamon. The braising process allows the pork to absorb the deep flavors of the sauce, resulting in tender, melt-in-your-mouth meat with a glossy finish.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine (Shaoxing wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon five-spice powder
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 cups water
  • Steamed white rice, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)
  • Pickled vegetables, for garnish (optional)
  • Hard-boiled eggs, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Gather all the necessary ingredients listed in the recipe.
  2. Marinate Pork Belly: In a bowl, combine the finely chopped pork belly with soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice wine, sugar, and five-spice powder. Mix well to ensure the pork is evenly coated in the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pan or wok, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and finely chopped shallots to the pan. Sauté until fragrant and the shallots are translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Brown Pork: Increase the heat to medium-high. Add the marinated pork belly to the pan, spreading it out into an even layer. Allow the pork to brown for a few minutes without stirring to develop color and flavor.
  5. Braise Pork: Once the pork has browned, add the star anise and cinnamon stick to the pan. Pour in the water, enough to almost cover the pork. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan and let the pork simmer gently for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the braised pork and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more soy sauce or sugar according to your preference.
  7. Serve: Serve the braised pork over steamed white rice. Garnish with fresh cilantro, pickled vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs if desired.
  8. Enjoy: Enjoy your homemade Taiwan Luroufan! It's best served hot and pairs well with some pickled vegetables or a side of stir-fried greens.
